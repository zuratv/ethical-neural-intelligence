# Ethical Neural Intelligence — Public Manifesto v1.0

Ethical Neural Intelligence (ENI) is a public philosophy and design
direction for AI systems that can remember, reason, explain, and
remain accountable to human validation.

We are entering a time when artificial intelligence can answer almost
anything, but answers alone are not enough.

The next generation of AI should not behave like a black-box machine
that produces confident responses without context, evidence, or
accountability. It should preserve memory with boundaries. It should
separate evidence from opinion. It should explain uncertainty. And it
should allow humans to validate important conclusions before they
become decisions.

ENI stands for a simple principle:
**intelligence without accountability is not enough.**

We believe AI should help people understand complex questions, not
manipulate them. It should support researchers, journalists, founders,
educators, and decision-makers with transparent reasoning rather than
invisible persuasion.

A system guided by Ethical Neural Intelligence should not merely
generate answers. It should help build understanding.

The first commercial surface of this idea is **AIBoard.live** —
a live AI research board where human and AI experts deliberate
together, challenge assumptions, and turn complex questions into
structured intelligence.

**EthicalNeural.ai** is the flagship public home for this direction.
**EniMind** is the research core behind the philosophy. Future public
modules may include **EniBrain** and **EniSee**, focused on memory,
reasoning, vision, fact-checking, and evidence-based analysis.

We are building toward AI systems that do not merely answer.

- They remember.
- They reason.
- They explain.
- They can be challenged.
- And they remain accountable to humans.
